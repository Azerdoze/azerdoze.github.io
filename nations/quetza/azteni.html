The Quepatec Azteni Empire

The land known today as Aztenia is what is left of the old empire. Controlling the region's core and the ancient capital of Zapotecla, these folk kept the great master alchemists of old, keeping with them the mastery of poisons. They mainly worship Quetza, the serpent queen, spirit of the jungle and the pantheon's leader.

In battle, the azteni are known for using blowguns to poison and enfeeble their foes from a distance, so they become easy prey for their sharp maquahitl.

The Great Deluge left this nation's territory into a swampy area of the jungle. The region, suffered a boom of amphibian fauna, from poisonous frogs to slippery gigantic snakes. As a result, the locals grew a fascination for these animals, taming them and using them in daily chores.
